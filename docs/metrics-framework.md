# SmartDrobe Metrics Framework

## Product Objective
To reduce decision fatigue and improve wardrobe utilization for our users through AI-assisted outfit recommendations and digital closet organization.

---

## North Star Metric
**Outfit Acceptance Rate (OAR)**
* **Definition:** The percentage of AI-recommended outfits that the user either "Saves", "Schedules", or explicitly marks as "Worn".
* **Why it matters:** It directly measures the core value proposition: the quality and relevance of the AI's styling decisions. A high OAR means the product is actively solving the user's "What do I wear?" problem.

---

## 1. Activation Metrics
* **Time to First Outfit (TTFO):** The average time from account creation to generating the first AI outfit suggestion.
* **Closet Critical Mass:** The percentage of users who upload at least 10 items to their digital closet within the first 3 days. (10 items is considered the minimum required for the AI to make meaningful combinations).

---

## 2. Engagement Metrics
* **Weekly Active Users (WAU):** Users who open the app and interact with a core feature (generate outfit, add item, view calendar) at least once in a 7-day period.
* **Generations per Session:** The average number of times a user clicks "Generate Outfit" per session. (A very high number might indicate the AI is missing the mark; a very low number might indicate low engagement. We are looking for the "Goldilocks" zone where the user finds a good outfit within 2-3 taps).
* **Calendar Utilization:** The percentage of WAUs who use the Outfit Calendar to plan looks in advance.

---

## 3. Retention Metrics
* **D30 Retention:** Percentage of users still actively using the app 30 days after signup.
* **Wardrobe Churn Rate:** The rate at which users delete items from their digital closet (can indicate decluttering, or conversely, items being worn out/discarded).

---

## 4. Monetization (Future Potential)
*(While currently a free utility, these metrics inform future revenue models)*
* **Affiliate Click-Through Rate:** If the AI suggests missing pieces (e.g., "You need white sneakers to complete this look"), the CTR on the purchasing link.
* **Premium Feature Conversion:** Rate of users upgrading to a hypothetical premium tier for advanced features like travel packing AI or unlimited closet capacity.
